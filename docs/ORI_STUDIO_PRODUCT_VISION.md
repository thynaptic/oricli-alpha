# ORI Studio Product Vision

## What ORI Studio is

ORI Studio is the small-business operator surface for ORI.

It is the place where a solo operator or tiny team sets up repeat work, runs it by email, gets briefings back, and stays on top of the business without learning another bloated tool.

ORI Studio is not the everyday companion layer. That is ORI Home.

ORI Studio is not the creative playground. That is ORI Dev later.

ORI Studio is the business-running product.

## Who it is for

ORI Studio is for:

- solo operators
- family businesses
- 1-2 person service companies
- tiny agencies
- consultants
- very small dev shops

Think:

- cleaning businesses
- plumbers
- HVAC companies
- property managers
- bookkeeping shops
- solo founders

These users do not want AI ideology. They want useful help, reliable routines, and less repetitive work.

## Core promise

The emotional promise is:

`ORI knows my business and handles it for me when I need her to.`

The functional promise is:

- build the workflow once
- run it by email or schedule
- get the result back in plain language
- keep moving

## Product principles

### 1. Email-first

Email is not a side channel. It is the main runtime surface.

Users should be able to:

- run automations by email
- receive results by email
- approve or reject work by email
- get daily briefings by email

The dashboard exists to set up and manage the work, not to force daily usage.

### 2. Small-business first

The product should be understandable to someone running a real business, not just to AI-native users.

That means:

- plain language
- practical workflow names
- fewer abstract choices
- more starter templates
- stronger defaults

### 3. Private by default

The customer-facing promise should be:

- private by default
- predictable pricing
- no pay-per-token billing anxiety

Internal language like `sovereign` can still exist in architecture and doctrine, but the public product should explain the value in normal language.

### 4. Business jobs over generic builders

Studio should lead with business jobs, not abstract workflow mechanics.

Good examples:

- Invoice check and summary
- Customer follow-up draft
- Morning business briefing
- Weekly job recap
- Daily lead digest
- Approval loop for outgoing messages

Bad first impressions:

- empty canvases
- too many step types
- DSL-first interfaces
- multiple overlapping workflow products

### 5. Calm, welcoming trust

The product should feel:

- calm
- clear
- trustworthy
- practical

It should not feel:

- ominous
- overly technical
- combative
- like a secret AI bunker

## Product boundaries

### ORI Studio

- SMB workflow operator surface
- setup, run, approve, monitor
- email-first
- business-first

### ORI Home

- everyday companion
- chat-centric
- more personal and ambient

### ORI Dev

- later creative companion
- builder / Dreamweaver-like product
- not the foundation product

Studio should not drift into Home or Dev territory.

## Canonical Studio workflow surface

`Automations` is the canonical workflow page for Studio.

It should be the clear place to:

- start from a business-ready template
- create or edit an automation
- choose email or schedule triggers
- understand how ORI will run it
- run it and monitor the result

The following are secondary or advanced surfaces:

- `Workflows`
- `Pipeline Canvas`
- `ORI Studio`

They may remain for power users or internal iteration, but they should not define the main SMB experience.

## UX implications

Studio should prefer:

- `Automations` over `Workflows`
- starter templates over blank slates
- business job names over abstract labels
- email and schedule triggers over advanced triggers
- simple operational status over technical execution details

Marketing pages should prefer:

- warm, welcoming palettes
- soft branding
- simple language

They should avoid:

- aggressive “AI category war” framing
- heavy use of internal philosophy language
- intimidating visual tone

## Product rule of thumb

If a small-business owner landed on the page and asked:

`What does this help me do this week?`

the product should answer that in under 10 seconds.

If it cannot, we are still being too abstract.
